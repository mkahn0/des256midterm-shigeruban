Personal Reflection

I chose to do this project on Shigeru Ban because I find his commitment to sustainable architecture and humanitarian projects inspiring. Ban proves that buildings do not necessarily need to be constructed with fancy or expensive materials, but that even common reusable materials, like paper, can create innovative forms with intentional function.

Ban’s work demonstrates the importance of thoughtful design, and how it can address problems, like those caused by crisis situations. He adapts his structures to assist with the needs of those who will use it, like the need for temporary housing with the Paper Log House, privacy with the Paper Partition System or a gathering space like with the Cardboard Cathedral. Ban’s projects are environmentally conscious while being socially impactful, introducing an intersectionality between the two seemingly unrelated goals.
